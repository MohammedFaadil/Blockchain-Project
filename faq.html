Frequently Asked Questions

What is Blockchain?

A blockchain is a decentralized digital ledger that securely records transactions in linked blocks, ensuring tamper-proof data.

How does Proof of Work secure the blockchain?

Proof of Work requires miners to solve a difficult cryptographic puzzle, making it expensive to alter blocks and ensuring network security.

What are Transactions in Blockchain?

Transactions are records of asset transfer between accounts, included inside blocks to update ownership or states.

What happens if someone tries to change a block?

Changing a block changes its hash, breaking the chain’s integrity and invalidating subsequent blocks, making tampering evident.

Can blockchain be centralized?

Technically yes, but one major benefit is decentralization where no single party controls the ledger, increasing transparency & security.

What is a Node in blockchain?

A node is a participant computer that maintains a copy of the blockchain and helps validate transactions and blocks.
